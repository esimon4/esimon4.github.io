Takeaway #1 ‚Äî Get to Know Your Users
Prior to my time at WINS, I figured most companies and startups made marketing and product decisions through more or less internal deliberation. But after working at WINS, I realized that every advertisement, product decision, brand positioning, and design choice is meticulously researched, tested, and iterated upon long before it's available to the public.

But how exactly do companies research, test, and iterate on these things? By talking to their users. With the help of firms like WINS, trendy crypto startups and established insurance giants alike spend considerable time and money getting to know who exactly is buying their products and using their services.

What I found particularly interesting was that companies were interested in their users beyond the ways in which they interacted with their products. Apple did not just care about why or how people used ApplePay ‚Äî they wanted to know about ApplePay users themselves. What are their pain points? What are their hobbies? What motivates them? This comprehensive approach to user research provided a truly holistic understanding of a given set of consumers, allowing companies to make more informed, calculated product decisions.
